Islamic Faith

Islam is a faith and comprehensive way of life that literally means 'peace through
submission to God.'' It provides a clear understanding of a person's relationship
with God, purpose in life, and ultimate destiny. A Muslim is someone who adopts
the Islamic way of life by believing in the Oneness of God and the prophethood
of Muhammad, peace be upon him (pbuh). Today, Islam is one of the fastest
growing religions and is practiced by more than 1.2 billion Muslims across the world.
The most essential principle in Islam is the purely monotheistic belief in one God.
God is the Creator of everything in the universe and is unique from His creation.
Muslims are encouraged to develop a direct and personal relationship with God
without any intermediaries. Muslims often refer to God as Allah, which simply
means “God” in the Arabic language. Islamically, God(Allah) has 99 names and
among those names, Allah is one of them and the first of all names. Allah describes
Himself in the holy book of muslims known as Quran, by stating: 
            "Say: He is Allah, the one and only.
             Allah, the eternal,absolute;
             He begettethnot, nor is he begotten,
             And there is none like unto him."
             (chapter 112 verse 1-4)

However, after the prophets delivered the divine guidance to their people, their message

In 610 C.E., Angel Gabriel visited Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) with

Muslims also believe that Allah sent revealed books as guidance to humanity through

  • The Zabur given to prophet Dawud
  • The Taurat(Torah) given to prophet Musa
  • The Injeel given to prophet Isa

The Quran is the last revelation from Allah, consisting of His literal speech. It confirms truths
from the previous scriptures and maintains the same core message of worshiping Allah and
living righteously. allah has ensured that the Quran is protected from corruption, safeguarding
it for all of humanity to benefit from until the end of time. It is the only holy book that has
been meticulously preserved in its original text.

The Five Pillars of Islam

Islam clearly adresses the real puporse of life. Allah declares in Quran, chapter 51 verse 56 

                    "I have  created jinns and 
                    mankind,only to worship me "
Worship in Islam is a comprehensive concept that urges people to be conscious of Allah throughout
their daily lives and provides a framework to help people live a balanced and virtuous life. Hence
the purpose of life islamically is worship Only and Only Allah the Creator of everything, without
associating Him with any other gods.

This way of life promotes strong moral character, good relations with people, and just
and harmonious societies. Devoting one's self to a life of submission to God is the key
to attaining a true sense of peace because it produces a balance of spiritual needs with
worldly affairs. It also lends special meaning to the concept of living one's life responsibly,
aware of the accountability to come in the hereafter. Islam has five pillars that all muslims
must follow as long as they're alive. and these pillars include:

  1. Shahada(Profession of Faith). Believing that there is non to worship but Allah and Prophet
    Muhammad is His messanger. in this pillar, muslims have to believe in the Oneness of Allah
    withought associating Him with any other gods and accepting thet prophet Muhammad is not
    more than His messanger.
  2. The Five Daily Prayers.These are the obligatory(compulsory) congregation prayers that are
    prformed daily. Muslims must pray at dawn, noon, mid-afternoon, after sunset and after dark.
    They must be performed in the mosqueOnly women are excused to perform these prayers
    inside their houses.
  3. Zakat(Alms). In accordance with Islamic law, Muslims donate a fixed portion of their income
    to community members in need. Many rulers and wealthy Muslims build mosques, drinking
    fountains, hospitals, schools, and other institutions both as a religious duty and to secure the
    blessings associated with charity.
  4. Fasting the Holy Month of RamadhanRamadhan is the 9th month on the islamic calender.
    Fasting is compulsary during this month to all healthy adult muslims, apart from the sick
    people that are out of their senses(mentally disturbed), and the old. This is a month of
    repentance and doing good, forexample, helping the needy, being kind and so on.
  5. Hajj(Pilgrimage).Hajj means visiting the holy mosque(kaaba) in mecca.Every healthy and
    financially stable muslim is supposed to visit the kaaba.
