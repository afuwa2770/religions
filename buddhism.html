Introduction

Buddhism was founded by Siddhartha Gautamawho later became Buddha.He lived during the 15th
century BC. He was born in a wealthy family as a prince in a country currently known as Nepal. Not
only having a good and easy life, Gautama was moved by suffering in the world. Gautama gave up his
lavish life and endured poverty. He promoted the idea of "Middle Way"( extreme asceticism or extreme
wealth) were the path to enlightment, but rather a way of life between the two xetremes. After six years
of searching, Buddhists believe Gautama found enlightenment while meditating under a Bodhi tree(the
tree of awakening). He spent the rest of his life teaching others about how to achieve this spiritual state.
When Gautama passed away around 483 B.C., his followers began to organize a religious movement.

Buddhist monasteries were built, and missionary work was encouraged. The Mahabodhi temple in Bihar
India- the site of enlightment, is now the major Buddhist pilgrimage site.

Buddhism Beliefs

The Budha taught about four norbel truths. The first trueth is called "suffering(dukkha)" which teaches
that everyone in life is suffering in some way. The second truth is "origin of suffering(Samudaya)", this
means that all sufferings come from desire(Tanha). The third trueth is "cessation of suffering(Noridha)".
and it is said that it is possible to stop suffering and achieve enlightment. The fourth truth is " path to the
cessation of suffering(Magga)", is about the "Middle Way" which are the steps to achieve enlightment.

Budhists believe in the wheel of rebirth,where souls are born again in two different bodies depending on
the way they conducted themselves in their previous lives. This is connected to "Karma", which refers to
a person's good or bad actions in their past life, can impact them in the future.

There are two main groups of Buddhism,"Mahayana Buddhism" and "Theravada Buddhism". Mahayana
Buddhism is common in Tibet, China, Taiwan, Japan and Mongolia. It emphasizes the role models of
bodhisattvas( beings that have achieved enlightment and return to teach humans). Theravada Buddhism
is common in Sri Lanka, Combodia, Thailand, Laos and Burma(Myanmar). It emphasizes a monastic lifestyle
and meditation,as the way to enlightment.

Buddhism has been a contrversial religion. The head of the Tibetan school of Buddhism and trditional leader
of Tibet, the Dlai Lama, fled from China-controlled Tibet in 1959 to India in fear of his life. Many Tibetan
Buddhists actively resist Chinese control of the region. Recently, the current Dalia Lama, who is understood
to be the fourteenth reincarnation of the first Dlai Lama, has raised questions over whether and where he will
choose to reincarnate.
