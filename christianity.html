Christianity and Beliefs

The Beginning of Christianity

Christianity traces its begginning to the miraculous, adult ministry, death and resurrection of Jesus Christ.
Over 2000 years ago in Palestine today known as Israel. Jesus was born into a humble jewish family and
his mother was a young peasant woman named Mary.

Christians believe that Jesus's father was the holy spirit of God which made him both fully human and fully
divine. Jesus's earliest followers came to believe that he was the Messiah sent by God to free God's people
from slavery, sin, and death.
Christians believe that God sent his son Jesus in human form, so that people would better understand that
God is a caring and loving parent.

Jesus lived and experienced the suffering of humans. He heald the sick and told stories, or parables and preached
sermons that taught what God wanted people to do, to love God with all their hearts and love their neighbours as
they love themselves. Jesus taught by example, by being loving and forgiving himself, he taught others by loving
and forgiving especially towards those who were considered outcasts in society.

During his adult ministry, Jesus built up a loyal following led by his twelve diciples. He also made enemies among
the political and religious leaders during his time. But in the end, these powerful leaders were threatened due the
growing following of Jesus that the Roman governer eneded up sentencing him to death and had him cricified.
On the third day after Jesus's death, his followers found his tomb empty and discovered that he has been raised from
the dead. Christians believe that the painful sacrifice of Jesus's life on the cross shows how much God loves His people.
Jesus paid with his life on earth ofr the sins of the world.

Christians beleive that God showed Jesus's message of love and forgiving was more powerful than death after raising
him from the dead, and that believeing in Jesus and following the example of his life and his teachings would lead to
eternal life after death. His ressurection is the sign of God's salvation offered to all people.

After his ressurection, Jesus Christ's followers spread his message throught the world, creating Christian Church. And
today there are about two billion Christian christians living all over the world, with the belief that Jesus is the son of
God(fully human and divine) and he died on the cross for the sins of the world.

  • Christians believe in the trinity(the three parts of God), God the father(creator), God the son(Jesus), and God the
    holy spirit(Sanctifier). The holy spirit representing God's presence in the world.
