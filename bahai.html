Bahai Faith

The word Bahai is either used as an adjective to refer to the Baha'i Faithor for a follower of Baha'u'llah.
The word is not a noun, meaning the religion as a whole. It is derived from the Arabic word Baha, meaning
"glory" or "splendour". The use of the term "Bahaism"(or Baha'ism) has been used in the past, but is fading
from use. Bahai Faith is a monotheistic religion that was founded by Baha'u'llah in Persia during the 19th century,
emphasizing unity with the spirit of all humankind. It is estimated that there are five to six million Baha'is around
the world in more than 200 countries.

Baha'i teachings emphasize unity of the major world religions. Religious history has been see to have unfolded
through a series of devine messengers, each establishing a religion suited to the needs of the time and
also the capacity of the people. These messengers include Abraham, Krishna, Budha, Jesus, Muhammad and others,
including the most recently Baha'u'llah in Bahai belief. Each messenger taught of the next, and Baha'u'llah's
life and teachings fulfill end-time promises of all previous scriptures. Humanity is understood to be involved in collective
evolution, and the need of the present time, is for the establishment of peace, justice and unity on a global scale.

Beliefs

Three core principles establish Bahai teachings and doctrine:

  • The unity of God
  • Religion
  • Of humankind
from these stems, comes the belief tha God reveals his wills through devine educators, with the purpose of transforming
the character of humankind and developing,within those who respond,moral and spiritual qualities. Religion is thus seen
as unified and progresive age to age.

Bahai writings describe a single inaccessible omniscient, omnipresent, and almighty God who created all things
in the universe. The existance of God and the universe is eternal , without beginning or end. Though inaccessible
directily, God is seen as conscious of creation, with a purpose that is expressed throuh messengers termed
manifestations of God.

Bahai teachings state that God is too great for humans to comprehend, or to create an accurate image of human
understanding of God is through the revelations and manifestations. In Bahai religion, there is a significant emphasis
on mmontheism.Their teachings state that the attributes applied to God are used to translate Godlines into
human terms and to help individuals concentrate on personal attributes in worshiping God, in order to develop potentialities
on their spiritual path. According to the teachings, human purpose is to learn,know and love God through prayer and reflection.
